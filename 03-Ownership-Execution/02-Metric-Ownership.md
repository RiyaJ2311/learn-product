# Metric Ownership

**Core Principle:** Own metrics end-to-end. What would you do as CEO of this metric?

---

## The Problem

PMs often "own" metrics but don't actually own them:
- They report on metrics but don't drive them
- They wait for others to fix problems
- They don't take full responsibility for outcomes

**Bad Pattern:**
> "The metric is down. Engineering needs to fix it."

**Good Pattern:**
> "The metric is down. Here's what I'm doing to fix it: [actions]. I'm working with engineering on [specific items]."

---

## The Framework

### Step 1: Define Ownership
For each metric you "own," ask:
- What does ownership really mean?
- What am I responsible for?
- What can I control vs influence?

### Step 2: The CEO Test
Ask yourself:
- What would I do as CEO of this metric?
- What actions would I take?
- What decisions would I make?

### Step 3: Take Action
Based on the CEO test:
- What can I do directly?
- What can I influence?
- What do I need from others?

### Step 4: Own the Outcome
- Take full responsibility
- Drive actions end-to-end
- Report on progress
- Learn from results

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Conversion Metric

**Bad Ownership:**
> "Conversion is down. Engineering needs to fix the checkout flow."

**Good Ownership:**
> "Conversion is down 5%. As owner of this metric, here's what I'm doing:
> - Investigating root cause (data analysis)
> - Working with engineering on checkout flow fixes
> - Testing alternative approaches
> - Reporting weekly on progress
> 
> I own the outcome, and I'm driving actions to fix it."

---

### Example 2: Retention Metric

**Bad Ownership:**
> "Retention is stable. No action needed."

**Good Ownership:**
> "Retention is stable at 40%, but our target is 50%. As owner of this metric, here's what I'm doing:
> - Analyzing cohorts to find improvement opportunities
> - Testing onboarding improvements
> - Working with product on feature adoption
> - Setting up weekly dashboard to track progress
> 
> I own driving this to 50%."

---

### Example 3: Feature Adoption Metric

**Bad Ownership:**
> "Feature adoption is 15%. Users need to discover it better."

**Good Ownership:**
> "Feature adoption is 15%, target is 25%. As owner of this metric, here's what I'm doing:
> - Improving discoverability (onboarding, prompts)

> - Analyzing which users adopt and why
> - Iterating based on feedback
> 
> I own getting this to 25%."

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Reporting Without Owning
**Problem:** Reporting on metrics but not driving them.
**Fix:** Use the CEO test. What would you do as CEO?

### Mistake 2: Blaming Others
**Problem:** "Engineering needs to fix this" or "Users need to..."
**Fix:** Own the outcome. Drive actions. Work with others.

### Mistake 3: Passive Ownership
**Problem:** "I own this metric" but no actions.
**Fix:** Take action. Drive improvements. Report on progress.

### Mistake 4: Not Tracking Progress
**Problem:** Owning metrics but not tracking progress.
**Fix:** Set up weekly dashboards. Report on progress. Learn from results.

---

## The CEO Test

**Question:** What would I do as CEO of this metric?

**Think:**
- What actions would I take?
- What decisions would I make?
- What resources would I allocate?
- How would I measure progress?

**Then:**
- Take those actions (within your scope)
- Make those decisions (permissionless execution)
- Allocate resources (work with others)
- Measure progress (weekly dashboards)

---

## Building the Habit

### Week 1: Awareness
- For each metric you own, ask: "What would I do as CEO?"
- Note when you're reporting without owning
- Catch yourself and take action

### Week 2: Practice
- Force yourself to take one action per metric per week
- Document your actions
- Self-grade: Did I own the outcome?

### Week 3: Refinement
- Make it automatic
- Build weekly dashboard habits
- Continue self-grading

## Advanced: End-to-End Ownership

True ownership means:
- **Defining:** What's the metric? What's the target?
- **Measuring:** How do we track it? What's the baseline?
- **Analyzing:** What's driving it? What's the root cause?
- **Acting:** What are we doing? What's the plan?
- **Learning:** What worked? What didn't? What's next?

### The "Own Without Asking" Test

Answer these questions for your primary metric (without looking at a dashboard):

1. **What's the current value?** ‚Üí If you don't know within 5%, you don't own it
2. **What was it last week?** ‚Üí If you can't recall, you're not tracking weekly
3. **What's driving the trend?** ‚Üí If you can't explain variance, you're reporting not owning
4. **What are you doing about it?** ‚Üí If no actions, you're monitoring not owning

**Score yourself:**
- 4/4 = True ownership
- 2-3/4 = Tracking but not owning
- 0-1/4 = Not owning (yet)

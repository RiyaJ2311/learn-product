# Impact Selling

**Core Principle:** Make wins stick. Repeat your wins enough times that they become part of the narrative.

---

## The Problem

PMs often achieve wins but don't make them stick. They mention them once and assume stakeholders remember. This leads to:
- Wins being forgotten
- Impact not being recognized
- Lack of credit for your work

**Bad Pattern:**
> "We improved engagement by 20%." [Mentioned once, then forgotten]

**Good Pattern:**
> "We improved engagement by 20%." [Repeated in multiple contexts until it sticks]

---

## The Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Win
What's the win?
- What did you achieve?
- What's the impact?
- Why does this matter?

### Step 2: Repeat Strategically
Don't repeat randomly. Repeat strategically:
- In relevant meetings
- In status updates
- In reviews
- In conversations

**Key:** Repeat in contexts where it matters.

### Step 3: Make It Stick
Repeat enough times that it becomes part of the narrative:
- First mention: Introduce the win
- Second mention: Reinforce the win
- Third mention: Make it part of the story
- Fourth+ mention: It's now part of the narrative

**Key:** Repetition builds memory.

### Step 4: Connect to Impact
Always connect wins to impact:
- What problem did this solve?
- What's the business impact?
- Why does this matter?

**Key:** Impact makes wins memorable.

## Examples

### Example 1: Feature Launch Win

**Win:** "We launched feature X, which increased engagement by 20%."

**Repetition Strategy:**
- **Week 1:** "We launched feature X. Early results show 20% engagement increase."
- **Week 2:** "Feature X is performing well. We're seeing 20% engagement increase."
- **Week 3:** "Feature X continues to drive results. 20% engagement increase."
- **Week 4:** "Feature X has been a success. 20% engagement increase."

**Result:** Win becomes part of the narrative.

---

### Example 2: Metric Improvement Win

**Win:** "We improved retention from 40% to 50%."

**Repetition Strategy:**
- **Status Update:** "We improved retention from 40% to 50%."
- **Review Meeting:** "One of our key wins: retention improved from 40% to 50%."
- **Stakeholder Update:** "Retention improvement: 40% to 50%."
- **Quarterly Review:** "This quarter, we improved retention from 40% to 50%."

**Result:** Win becomes part of the narrative.

---

### Example 3: Problem-Solving Win

**Win:** "We solved the checkout problem, which increased conversion by 15%."

**Repetition Strategy:**
- **Problem-Solving Context:** "We solved the checkout problem. Conversion increased 15%."
- **Impact Context:** "Our checkout improvements drove 15% conversion increase."
- **Success Story Context:** "One success: solving checkout problem, 15% conversion increase."
- **Narrative Context:** "We've been focused on conversion. Our checkout improvements drove 15% increase."

**Result:** Win becomes part of the narrative.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Mentioning Once
**Problem:** Mentioning wins once and assuming stakeholders remember.
**Fix:** Repeat wins strategically until they stick.

### Mistake 2: Not Connecting to Impact
**Problem:** "We did X" without explaining why it matters.
**Fix:** Always connect wins to impact.

### Mistake 3: Over-Repeating
**Problem:** Repeating wins too much, becoming annoying.
**Fix:** Repeat strategically, not constantly.

### Mistake 4: Not Adapting Message
**Problem:** Using the same message in all contexts.
**Fix:** Adapt the message to the context while keeping the core win.

## Repetition Strategy

### When to Repeat
- **Relevant Meetings:** When the win is relevant to the discussion
- **Status Updates:** Regular updates where wins fit naturally
- **Reviews:** Performance reviews, quarterly reviews
- **Conversations:** When the topic comes up naturally

### How to Repeat
- **First Mention:** Introduce the win clearly
- **Second Mention:** Reinforce the win with context
- **Third Mention:** Make it part of the story
- **Fourth+ Mention:** It's now part of the narrative

### What to Repeat
- **The Win:** What did you achieve?
- **The Impact:** Why does this matter?
- **The Context:** How does this fit into the bigger picture?

### Example: Translating to Leadership Language

**Win:** "Profile completion improved by 12%"

**Team Language:**
> "Profile completion is up 12%. Our tooltip experiment worked."

**Leadership Language:**
> "We improved activation funnel efficiency by 12%, which projects to 50 more activated merchants per month. At $45 ARPU, that's ~$2K additional MRR."

**Key Takeaway:** Leadership cares about business impact (revenue, retention, growth), not feature metrics. Always translate.

### Anti-Pattern: The "Humble Achiever"

**Problem:** You ship good work but feel uncomfortable "bragging" about it.

**Reframe:** Impact selling isn't bragging. It's:
1. Giving leadership visibility into what's working
2. Building credibility for future investments
3. Helping the team get credit
4. Creating documentation for performance reviews

**If you don't sell your impact, no one will.**
